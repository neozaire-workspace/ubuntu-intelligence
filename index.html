Founder's Note

The Problem Was Never Lack Of Intelligence.

"I am because we are."

Ubuntu — The founding principle
Zimbabwe → United Kingdom → Africa

Neo Zaire · Founder · Ubuntu Intelligence

I was not born in a boardroom, a think tank, or a venture capital office. My journey began in Zimbabwe, where I learned early that survival, innovation, and community are inseparable.

Africa is rich in people, culture, minerals, and ideas — yet poor in systems, information, and coordinated decision-making. The problem was never a lack of African intelligence. It was a lack of intelligence infrastructure.

Governments make decisions without real data. Investors operate without clear visibility. Citizens lack access to transparent information. Diaspora communities are disconnected from local development insights.

Meanwhile, the world is entering an era where data and artificial intelligence guide economies. Africa — with 1.4 billion people — cannot afford to be a passive consumer of these systems. It must build its own.

Ubuntu Intelligence is that beginning. An autonomous system that collects data, analyses trends, generates insights, and publishes intelligence — 24 hours a day, built by an African, for Africa.
